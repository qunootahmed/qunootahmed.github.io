    Ruronzi - Rwanda
    Educate to Empower
    Ruronzi, cultivated and exported by Ikawa House since 2020, embodies their drive for innovation and coffee education in Rwanda. Their first export showcases this commitment. Learn more
    Kilimanjaro - Tanzania
    Sharing is Caring
    Discover Kilimanjaro coffee, crafted with care by Mamsera, a cooperative boasting one of the highest womens participation rates in the country. Sourced by RichlandHub, an importer who believes sharing is caring, each cup supports community impact, giving back, and empowering women through the dedicated Women's Group behind this exceptional lot. Learn more
    Nova - Rwanda
    A collaborative cup
    With 2,800 smallholder farms, Nova Coffee grows red Arabica Bourbon in the volcanic soils of northern Rwanda. The beans are then exported by Africa-based, women-led Ikawa House. In every sip, discover delightful complexity and the shared fruits of partnership. Learn more
    Jebena Blend - Ethiopia/Cameroon
    Dark Chocolate Experience
    Named for the traditional pot used in the Ethiopian and Eritrean coffee ceremony, Jebena is a blend of organic beans roasted long and hot for a full body and bittersweet notes of dark chocolate and caramel. Learn more
    Bereka Blend - Ethiopia/Cameroon
    A blessed brew
    Meaning to be blessed in Amharic, Bereka celebrates the social ties innate to the East African coffee ceremony. This organic, medium roast blend warms the spirit with notes of baking spices and cane sugar sweetness. Proceeds go to Vision House, breaking the cycle of homelessness for families with children. Learn more

    Coffee Ceremony

    Join us at our Renton Location where the ceremony begins with pan roasting green (unroasted) coffee beans, which are then brewed in a clay pot known as a Jebena. You will get to hear the origins of coffee, taste one of the oldest techniques for brewing coffee, and snack on traditional treats.

    Seattle born. African roots.
    Bridging Cultures
    Bridging Cultures
    Boon Boona is inspired by our founders travels and family ties in Eritrea. Were helping Seattle discover the unique coffee and traditions of East Africa, where pouring a daily cup means taking time to connect.
    Reimagining the industry
    Reimagining the industry
    Beyond ethically sourcing the best beans, were shaping a more equitable coffee economy. We partner exclusively with African producers and exporters, send a larger share of profits to mainly women-owned growers, and open coffee lovers eyes to vibrant but overlooked markets.
    Cultivating community
    Cultivating community
    Our cafes are intentionally inclusive, serving as gathering places and welcoming venues for pop-up opportunities for artists, non-profits, and local, loved small businesses.
